Stephen R. Covey’s book 7 habits of highly effective people covers 7 habits you can incorporate into your life to help you achieve your goals and they are as follows:

1. Be proactive. In this habit Covey explains the fact that in order to achieve your goals you need to be in control of your actions and he suggests that in order to do so you must limit your reactionary decisions (decisions made as a result of external circumstances) and focus on things in your “Circle of Influence” or things in life that you can control.

2. Begin with the end in mind. This habit has an individual clearly define their goals personal and professional and create a mission statement connected to said goal that outlines your values and principals while keeping your goal in view creating a blueprint for you that will help guide your decisions and actions towards achieving said goal.

3. Put first things first. This habit builds on the work you have done as part of “Begin with the end in mind” and has you complete tasks not in order of urgency but in order of significance with regards to the goals you have created. In order to do this Covey also explains that in some cases you may need to say no to distractions and less important tasks in order to allow you to focus on your goals.
